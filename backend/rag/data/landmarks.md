# Major Landmarks in Sri Lanka

## Sigiriya Rock Fortress
Sigiriya, often referred to as the "Eighth Wonder of the World," is an ancient palace and fortress complex built on a massive column of rock standing nearly 200 metres high. Built by King Kasyapa in the 5th century, it features remnants of a ruined palace on the summit, the famous "Lion Gate," and a mirror wall adorned with ancient frescoes of celestial maidens. The climb involves about 1,200 steps and can be strenuous in the midday sun. It's best to visit early in the morning to avoid the heat and the crowds. Beware of the resident monkeys—they are known to snatch food and shiny objects from tourists.

## The Temple of the Sacred Tooth Relic (Kandy)
Located in the heart of Kandy, the Sri Dalada Maligawa (Temple of the Sacred Tooth Relic) is one of the most sacred places of worship in the Buddhist world. It houses the relic of the tooth of the Buddha. The best time to visit is during the daily offerings or "pujas," accompanied by traditional drumming. Visitors must wear clothes that cover shoulders and knees, and shoes must be deposited at the entrance. The temple is especially spectacular during the Esala Perahera, a grand festival held in July or August.

## Galle Fort
Built first by the Portuguese and then extensively fortified by the Dutch during the 17th century, Galle Fort is a UNESCO World Heritage site on the southwest coast. It is a beautiful, walkable walled city featuring narrow streets, colonial architecture, boutique shops, and cafes. Watching the sunset from the fort walls is a popular activity. The area is relatively relaxed regarding dress codes compared to religious sites, but modest beachwear is still appreciated when walking around town.

## Anuradhapura
Anuradhapura was the first capital of ancient Sri Lanka and remains one of the oldest continuously inhabited cities in the world. It is famous for its well-preserved ruins of ancient Sri Lankan civilization, including massive dagobas (brick stupas), ancient pools, and crumbling temples. The site is vast, so hiring a bicycle or a tuk-tuk is the best way to explore it. The sacred Bodhi Tree (Jaya Sri Maha Bodhi), grown from a cutting of the tree under which the Buddha attained enlightenment, is a central pilgrimage site here.

## Dambulla Cave Temple
The Dambulla Cave Temple, also known as the Golden Temple of Dambulla, is the largest and best-preserved cave temple complex in Sri Lanka. The complex consists of five separate caves containing over 150 stunning statues and intricate paintings related to Gautama Buddha and his life. The caves are located at the top of a 160-metre rock, requiring a short but steep hike. Tickets must be purchased at the bottom of the hill before starting the climb.
