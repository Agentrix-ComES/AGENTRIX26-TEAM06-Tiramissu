# Essential and Emergency Phrases in Sri Lanka

## Greetings and Politeness
- **Hello / Welcome:** Ayubowan (May you have a long life)
- **Thank you:** Istuti (is-TOO-tee)
- **Yes:** Ow
- **No:** Naa
- **Please:** Karunakara
- **Do you speak English?:** Oya ingrisi katha karanawada?

## Transport and Negotiation
- **How much is this?:** Meeka kiyada? (Me-ka ki-ya-da)
- **That is too expensive:** Eka godak wadi (E-ka go-dak wa-di)
- **Can you give me a better price?:** Mata aduiyak denne puluwan da?
- **Where are you going?:** Koheda yanné?
- **Does this bus go to [Destination]?:** Me bus eka [Destination] yannawa da?
- **Please use the meter:** Karunakara meter eka danna
- **Stop here, please:** Methana nawaththanna

## Food and Dining
- **Do you have food?:** Kanna deyak tiyenawa da?
- **I don't eat meat:** Mama mas kanne naa
- **Water, please:** Wathura denna
- **Not spicy, please:** Miris aduwen danna (or just say "No spice")
- **Delicious:** Hari rasai
- **The bill, please:** Bill eka denna

## Emergencies and Directions
- **Help!:** Udaw karanna!
- **Call the police:** Police-yata katha karanna
- **I need a doctor:** Mata dosthara kenek ona
- **Where is the hospital?:** Rohala koheda thiyenne?
- **Where is the bathroom?:** Washroom eka koheda thiyenne?
- **I am lost:** Mata paara waradila
- **Leave me alone:** Mata paaduwe inna denna (Useful if being harassed by touts)

*Note: While Sinhala is spoken by the majority of the population, Tamil is also widely spoken, especially in the north and east. However, basic English is understood in almost all tourist areas, hotels, and restaurants.*
