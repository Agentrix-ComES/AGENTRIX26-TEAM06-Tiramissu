# Transport in Sri Lanka

## Tuk-Tuks (Three-Wheelers)
Tuk-tuks are the most common way to get around cities and towns. Always negotiate the fare before getting in unless the tuk-tuk has a working meter. A standard rate is usually around 60 to 80 LKR per kilometer, but tourists are often quoted much higher. When bargaining, ask for a 30% to 40% discount off the initial price. Apps like PickMe and Uber are available in Colombo, Kandy, and major tourist hubs, offering fixed, metered rates and saving you the hassle of negotiation. 

## Trains
Sri Lankan trains offer some of the most scenic journeys in the world, especially the route from Kandy to Ella. However, they are frequently delayed, sometimes by 1 to 3 hours, especially in the hill country. Tickets for reserved seating (1st and 2nd class) must be booked weeks in advance. Unreserved 2nd and 3rd class tickets can be bought on the day of travel but expect crowded conditions. If a train is cancelled, the best alternative is usually heading to the nearest bus stand where private buses operate on the same routes.

## Buses
Buses are cheap and frequent. There are two main types: public CTB buses (red) and private buses (usually white with colourful decorations and loud music). Private buses are faster but can be very crowded and drive erratically. For longer routes, look for air-conditioned mini-buses (sometimes called 'luxury' or 'intercity' buses), which are more comfortable and only slightly more expensive. Always keep small change for bus fares.

## Private Vans and Taxis
For longer trips where trains or buses are inconvenient, you can hire a private car or van with a driver. This is a very common and comfortable way for tourists to travel. Negotiate a daily rate which should include the driver's food and accommodation (though many hotels provide driver quarters for free).
