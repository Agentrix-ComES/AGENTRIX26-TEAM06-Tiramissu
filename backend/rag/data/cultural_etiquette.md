# Cultural Etiquette in Sri Lanka

## Temple Rules
When visiting Buddhist and Hindu temples, strict dress codes apply. Both men and women must wear clothing that covers their shoulders and knees. You will be required to remove your shoes and hats before entering the sacred areas of the temple. It is a good idea to bring a pair of socks if you are visiting during the day, as the stone or sand floors can get incredibly hot under the sun. 

## Photography and Buddha Statues
Never pose for a photograph with your back to a Buddha statue; this is considered highly disrespectful. If you want to take a picture of a statue, do so while facing it, and do not include humans posing in front of it. Taking photos of monks should only be done after asking for permission. Avoid wearing clothing or having visible tattoos depicting the Buddha or other religious deities, as this has previously led to tourists being denied entry to the country or even deported.

## Greetings and Interaction
The traditional greeting in Sri Lanka is to press your palms together in front of your chest and say "Ayubowan," which means "May you have a long life." When interacting with monks, show extra respect. If you are offering something or receiving something, it is polite to use your right hand or both hands. Pointing with your index finger is considered rude; instead, use your whole hand or a slight nod of the head.

## Public Affection and Modesty
Public displays of affection, such as kissing or hugging, are frowned upon in Sri Lankan culture. While holding hands is generally acceptable in tourist areas, it's best to be conservative. Away from the beaches, women should avoid wearing overly revealing clothing to respect local sensibilities.

## Tipping
Tipping is widely expected in Sri Lanka. A 10% service charge is often added to bills in restaurants and hotels, but it is customary to leave a small extra tip if the service was good. Porters usually expect around 100 LKR per bag, and leaving a few hundred rupees a day for hotel housekeeping is appreciated. If you hire a driver for several days, a tip of 1000 to 2000 LKR per day is standard.
